Definition 1.1.8. Principal square root function.

Given any nonnegative real number \(x\geq 0\text{,}\) we denote by \(\sqrt{x}\) its unique nonnegative square root, and we call \(\sqrt{x}\) the principal square root of \(x\) (or more casually, the radical of \(x\)).

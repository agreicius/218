Definition 1.1.17. Set union and intersection.

Let \(A\) and \(B\) be subsets of a common set \(X\text{.}\)
  • The union of \(A\) and \(B\text{,}\) denoted \(A\cup B\) is defined as the set of all elements of \(X\) that lie either in \(A\) or \(B\text{:}\) i.e.,
    \begin{equation*} A\cup B=\{x\in X\mid x\in A \text{ or } x\in B\}\text{.} \end{equation*}

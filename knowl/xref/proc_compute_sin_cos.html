Procedure 2.16.23. Computing \(\cos\) and \(\sin\).

Given \(\theta\in \R\text{,}\) compute \(\cos\theta\) and \(\sin\theta\) as follows.
  1. Make a unit circle picture as in Figure 2.16.22. In more detail:
    1. Draw the unit circle \(x^2+y^2=1\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    2. Draw an oriented angle in standard position with radian measure \(\theta\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    3. Draw the point \(P=(x,y)\) where the terminal edge of your angle intersects the unit circle.
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗
    🔗
  2. Determine the values of the coordinates of \(P=(x,y)\) and conclude
    \begin{align*} \cos\theta \amp = x \amp \sin\theta\amp= y\text{.} \end{align*}

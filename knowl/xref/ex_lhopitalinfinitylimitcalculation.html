Example 7.2.9.

Use LH么pitals rule to calculate the limit of \(\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{x^2}{2^x-1}\text{.}\)
Solution.
The limit \(\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{x^2}{2^x-1}\) is of the indeterminate form \(\frac{\infty}{\infty}\text{,}\) so LH么pitals rule applies. Again, we replace the numerator and denominator of the quoitent with their derivatives:
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{x^2}{2^x-1}\amp = \frac{\infty}{\infty}\\ \amp \stackrel[L'H]{}=\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{\frac{d}{dx}(x^2)}{\frac{d}{dx}(2^x-1)}\\ \amp =\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{2x}{(\ln2)2^x}\\ \amp =\frac{\infty}{\infty}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
Unfortunately, our application of LH么pitals rule was not sufficient to resolve the limit here, because if we let \(x\to\infty\text{,}\) we have \(2x\to\infty\) and \((\ln2)2^x\to\infty\text{,}\) so our limit is still in the indeterminate form \(\frac{\infty}{\infty}\text{.}\) However, this means that we can apply LH么pitals rule again!
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{2x}{(\ln2)2^x}\amp = \frac{\infty}{\infty}\\ \amp \stackrel[L'H]{}=\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{\frac{d}{dx}(2x)}{\frac{d}{dx}((\ln2)2^x)}\\ \amp =\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{2}{(\ln2)^22^x}\\ \amp = \frac{2}{\infty}\\ \amp = 0 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
As \(x\to\infty\) the denominator of this limit approaches \(\infty\) while the numerator is equal to \(2\text{,}\) hence the limit goes to 0. Combining these two applications of LH么pitals rule, we see that the original limit is equal to zero:
\begin{equation*} \displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{x^2}{2^x-1}=0\text{.} \end{equation*}

Definition 3.4.12. Finite Limit at Infinity.

If the values of a function \(f(x)\) approach \(L\) as \(x\) grows without bound, then we say that \(L\) is the limit of \(f(x)\) as \(x\) approaches \(\infty\) and we write
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to\infty} f(x) =L \end{equation*}

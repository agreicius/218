Now \(\cos\theta\) is the horizontal coordinate of the point on the unit circle corresponding to \(\theta\text{,}\) or, in terms of "adjacent over hypotenuse," we have
\begin{equation*} \cos\theta = \frac{\sqrt{1-x^2}}{1} = \sqrt{1-x^2} \end{equation*}

\begin{equation*} \cos(\sin^{-1}x) = \sqrt{1-x^2} \end{equation*}

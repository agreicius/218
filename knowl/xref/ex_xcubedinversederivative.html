Example 8.8.8.

The function \(f(x) = x^3\) is one-to-one and has inverse function \(f^{-1}(x) = \sqrt[3]{x}\text{.}\) The point \((2,2^3) = (2,8)\) is on the graph of \(f(x)\) while the point \((8,\sqrt[3]{8}) = (8,2)\) is on the graph of \(f^{-1}(x)\text{.}\) What we have just found out is that the slope of the tangent line to \(f(x) = x^3\) at \(x=2\) is the reciprocal of the slope of the tangent line to the graph of \(f^{-1}(x) = \sqrt[3]{x}\) at \(x=8\text{.}\)

Theorem 1.6.21. Factoring tools.

  • Difference of powers.
    For all \(a,b\in \R\text{,}\) and all integers \(n\geq 2\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{equation} a^n-b^n=(a-b)(a^{n-1}+a^{n-2}b+\cdots+ab^{n-2}+b^{n-1})\text{.}\tag{1.44} \end{equation}
    In particular, we have
    \begin{align*} a^2-b^2 \amp =(a-b)(a+b) \\ a^3-b^3 \amp =(a-b)(a^2+ab+b^2)\\ a^4-b^4 \amp =(a-b)(a^3+a^2b+ab^2+b^2)\text{.} \end{align*}

  • Polynomial expression of \(x^m\).
    If we can write a polynnomial \(f(x)\) as
    \begin{equation*} f(x)=g(x^m) \end{equation*}
    for some polynomial \(g\text{,}\) then a factorization
    \begin{equation*} g(x)=p(x)q(x) \end{equation*}
    of \(g\) gives rise to the factorization
    \begin{equation*} f(x)=p(x^m)q(x^m)\text{.} \end{equation*}

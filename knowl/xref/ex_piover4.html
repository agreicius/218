The green triangle drawn in Figure 2.17.7 above is often referred to as a reference triangle. This is a 45-45-90 triangle so we know from [cross-reference to target(s) "thm_trianglesidelengths" missing or not unique] that the lengths of both legs are \(x=y=\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\text{.}\) Thus the coordinates of the red point are \((x,y) = (\cos(\pi/4),\sin(\pi/4))\text{.}\) This implies that
\begin{equation*} x = \cos(\pi/4) = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \text{ and } y = \sin(\pi/4) = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \end{equation*}
are the values of \(\cos(\pi/4)\) and \(\sin(\pi/4)\text{.}\)

Example 4.6.12.

The derivative of
\begin{equation*} y=4(2x+1)^3+5x \end{equation*}
is
\begin{equation*} y' = 4(3)(2x+1)^2\cdot(2)+5 = 24(2x+1)^2+5 \end{equation*}

We use the chain rule in the first term, where the outside function is \(f(u) = 4u^3\) with derivative \(f'(u) = 4(3u^2)\) and the inside function is \(u(x) = 2x+1\) with derivative \(u'(x) = 2\text{.}\) The second term is \(5x\) and has derivative \(5\text{,}\) which we calculate separately using the sum rule.

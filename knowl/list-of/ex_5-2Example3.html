\begin{equation*} h(x) = \frac{x^3-4x}{x^2-1} \end{equation*}

First, note that
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to\infty} h(x) = \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{x^3-4x}{x^2-1} = \frac{\infty}{\infty} \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to-\infty} h(x) = \lim_{x\to-\infty}\frac{x^3-4x}{x^2-1} = \frac{-\infty}{\infty} \end{equation*}
are both indeterminate. Here are the same two methods as in ExampleÂ 3.6.18. We only really need one but give both so that you can see more examples of using each.
ðŸ”—
Method 1: Divide the numerator and denominator by \(x^2\text{,}\) the highest power of \(x\) that appears in the denominator. This is equivalent to multiplying the numerator and denominator by \(1/x^2\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{x^3-4x}{x^2-1} \amp = \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{x^3-4x}{x^2-1}\cdot\frac{1/x^2}{1/x^2}\\ \amp = \lim_{x\to \infty}\frac{x-\frac{4}{x}}{1-\frac{1}{x^2}}\\ \amp = \frac{\infty-0}{1-0}\\ \amp = \frac{\infty}{1}\\ \amp =\infty \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
and similarly
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}\lim_{x\to-\infty}\frac{x^3-4x}{x^2-1} \amp = \lim_{x\to-\infty}\frac{x^3-4x}{x^2-1}\cdot\frac{1/x^2}{1/x^2}\\ \amp = \lim_{x\to -\infty}\frac{x-\frac{4}{x}}{1-\frac{1}{x^2}}\\ \amp = \frac{-\infty-0}{1-0}\\ \amp = \frac{-\infty}{1}\\ \amp =-\infty \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
Method 2: Factor \(x^3\) from the numerator and factor \(x^2\) from the denominator. Notice in this case that not all of the \(x\)â€™s cancel.
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{x^3-4x}{x^2-1} \amp = \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{x^3(1-\frac{4}{x^2})}{x^2(1-\frac{1}{x^2})}\\ \amp = \lim_{x\to \infty}\frac{x(1-\frac{4}{x^2})}{1-\frac{1}{x^2}}\\ \amp = \frac{\infty\cdot (1-0)}{1-0}\\ \amp = \frac{\infty}{1}\\ \amp =\infty \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
and similarly
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}\lim_{x\to-\infty}\frac{x^3-4x}{x^2-1} \amp = \lim_{x\to-\infty}\frac{x^3(1-\frac{4}{x^2})}{x^2(1-\frac{1}{x^2})}\\ \amp = \lim_{x\to -\infty}\frac{x(1-\frac{4}{x^2})}{1-\frac{1}{x^2}}\\ \amp = \frac{-\infty\cdot (1-0)}{1-0}\\ \amp = \frac{-\infty}{1}\\ \amp =-\infty \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
From both methods, we see that \(h(x)\) does NOT have any horizontal asymptotes, since the limits as \(x\to\infty\) and \(x\to-\infty\) are not finite.

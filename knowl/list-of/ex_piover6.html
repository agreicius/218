Example 2.17.7.

The angle \(\pi/6\) radians, drawn in standard position, intersects the unit circle at the point \((\cos(\pi/6),\sin(\pi/6))\) as shown below in Figure 2.17.8:
🔗
The green reference triangle in Figure 2.17.8 is a \(30-60-90\) triangle so we know from Theorem 2.17.1 that \(x = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\) and \(y = \frac{1}{2}\text{.}\) Thus \((x,y) = (\cos(\pi/6),\sin(\pi/6))\text{.}\) This implies that
\begin{equation*} x = \cos(\pi/6) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \text{ and } y = \sin(\pi/6) = \frac{1}{2} \end{equation*}
are the values of \(\cos(\pi/6)\) and \(\sin(\pi/6)\text{.}\)

Theorem 3.5.9. Indeterminate Forms for Algebraic Functions.

Each of the following is an indeterminate form, meaning that a limit in one of these forms may or may not exist, depending on the situation:
\begin{equation*} \frac{0}{0}\qquad \frac{\infty}{\infty}\qquad 0\cdot\infty\qquad \infty-\infty \end{equation*}

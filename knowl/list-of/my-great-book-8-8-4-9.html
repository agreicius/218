Example 6.7.12.

Find the derivative of \(f(x)=e^{x^2}\ln x\text{.}\)

Solution.
Using the product and chain rules, we have
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}\frac{d}{dx}\left(e^{x^2}\ln x\right)\amp =\frac{d}{dx}\left(e^{x^2}\right)\cdot\ln x+e^{x^2}\frac{d}{dx}(\ln x)\\ \amp =2xe^{x^2}\ln x+e^{x^2}\ \frac{1}{x}\\ \amp =e^{x^2}\left(2x\ln x+\frac{1}{x}\right) \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

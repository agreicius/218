Recall that tables are a convenient way of summarizing corresponding inputs and outputs of a function. For instance, we know that \(f(1977)=87\text{,}\) meaning that in 1977, there were 87 operating drive-in movie theaters. On the other hand, given the data available, it is accurate to say that when there were 87 operating movie theaters, the year was 1977. In terms of functions, we can write \(f^{-1}(87) = 1977\text{.}\) Is this saying anything meaningfully different? No. Remember: inverse functions are just a change in perspective.

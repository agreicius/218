Solution.
  1. We have \(f(x)=p(x)/q(x)\text{,}\) where
    \begin{align*} p(x) \amp = x-3 \amp q(x)\amp =x^2-4x-3=(x-1)(x-3)\text{.} \end{align*}
    Since the zeros of \(q\) are \(1\) and \(3\text{,}\) the implied domain \(D\) of \(f\) is
    \begin{equation*} D=\R-\{1,3\}=(-\infty,1)\cup (1,3)\cup (3,\infty)\text{.} \end{equation*}
    The set of zeros \(Z\) of \(f\) is the set of \(x\) where \(p(x)=0\) and \(q(x)\ne 0\text{.}\) Since \(p\) is zero only at \(x=3\text{,}\) and since \(3\) is a zero of \(q\text{,}\) we conclude that \(f\) has no zeros: i.e.,
    \begin{equation*} Z=\{\}=\emptyset\text{.} \end{equation*}

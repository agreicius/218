Example 2.17.11.

With a calculator in radian mode we can approximate \(\cos\left(-\frac{\pi}{5}\right)\approx 0.809\text{.}\) We can verify that this makes sense with a picture. As shown in the picture below, it does seem realistic that the angle \(-\frac{\pi}{5}\) (which is one-fifth of the way clockwise on the bottom half of the unit circle) meets the unit circle at a point whose \(x\)-coordinate is approximately \(0.809\) (relatively close to \(1\)).

  4. We check to see whether \(f(x)\) is even or odd by simplifying \(f(-x)\) and seeing if this is equal to either \(f(x)\) or \(-f(x)\text{.}\)

    We can see that \(f(-x)\neq f(x)\text{,}\) so \(f(x)\) is not even and does not have reflectional symmetry across the \(y\)-axis. \(f(-x)\neq -f(x)\) as well since \(-f(x) = -x^4-4x^3+16x\text{,}\) so \(f(x)\) is also not odd and does not have \(180^\circ\) rotational symmetry about the origin. \(f(x)\) is neither even nor odd and there are no helpful symmetries that we can use to save us time in sketching the graph.

  6. The end-behavior of \(f(x)\) is found by computing the limits at \(\pm\infty\text{,}\) which both result in a limit form of \(\infty-\infty\text{,}\) and so we force-factor. Thus
    \begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to\infty} (x^4+4x^3-16x) = \lim_{x\to\infty} x^4\left(1+\frac{4}{x}-\frac{16}{x^3}\right) = (\infty)^4(1+0-0) = \infty \end{equation*}

Example 6.6.20.

Find the derivative of the function
\begin{equation*} f(x) = \frac{5^{3x}}{1+x} \end{equation*}

Starting with the quotient rule, we have
\begin{align*} \frac{d}{dx}\left(\frac{5^{3x}}{1+x}\right) \amp = \frac{(1+x)\cdot \frac{d}{dx}(5^{3x})-5^{3x}\cdot\frac{d}{dx}(1+x)}{(1+x)^2} \amp \leftarrow\amp \text{ quotient rule }\\ \amp = \frac{(1+x)\cdot(\ln 5)5^{3x}(3)-5^{3x}\cdot(1)}{(1+x)^2} \amp \leftarrow \amp \text{ chain, exponential rules }\\ \amp = \frac{(3(\ln 5)(1+x)-1)5^{3x}}{(1+x)^2} \amp \leftarrow\amp \text{ simplify a bit by factoring \(5^{3x}\)} \end{align*}

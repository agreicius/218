Solution.
  1. To convert from radians to degrees, we multiply by the conversion factor \(\tfrac{180^\circ}{\pi \text{ rad}}\text{:}\)
    \begin{align*} \frac{\pi}{2} \text{ rad} \amp = \frac{\pi}{2}\cdot \frac{180^\circ}{\pi \text{ rad}}=90^\circ\\ \pi \text{ rad} \amp = \pi \cdot \frac{180^\circ}{\pi \text{ \rad}}=180^\circ \\ 2\pi \text{ rad} \amp = 2\pi \cdot \frac{180^\circ}{\pi \text{ \rad}}=360^\circ\\ -\frac{3\pi}{4} \text{ rad} \amp = -\frac{3\pi}{4}\cdot \frac{180^\circ}{\pi \text{ rad}}=-135^\circ\\ \frac{11\pi}{6} \text{ rad} \amp = \frac{11\pi}{6}\cdot \frac{180^\circ}{\pi \text{ rad}}=330^\circ\\ -\frac{2\pi}{3} \text{ rad} \amp = -\frac{2\pi}{3}\cdot \frac{180^\circ}{\pi \text{ rad}}=-120^\circ\text{.} \end{align*}
    🔗
    🔗
  2. To convert from degrees to radians, we multiply by the conversion factor \(\tfrac{\pi \text{ rad}}{180^\circ}\text{:}\)
    \begin{align*} 270^\circ \amp = 270^\circ \cdot \frac{\pi \text{ rad}}{180^\circ}=\frac{3\pi}{2} \text{ rad}\\ 225^\circ \amp = 225^\circ \cdot \frac{\pi \text{ rad}}{180^\circ}=\frac{5\pi}{4} \text{ rad}\\ -15^\circ \amp = -15^\circ \cdot \frac{\pi \text{ rad}}{180^\circ}=-\frac{\pi}{12} \text{ rad}\\ -360^\circ \amp = -360^\circ \cdot \frac{\pi \text{ rad}}{180^\circ}=-2\pi \text{ rad}\\ 210^\circ \amp = 210^\circ \cdot \frac{\pi \text{ rad}}{180^\circ}=\frac{7\pi}{6} \text{ rad}\\ -165^\circ \amp = -165^\circ \cdot \frac{\pi \text{ rad}}{180^\circ}=-\frac{11\pi}{12} \text{ rad}\text{.} \end{align*}

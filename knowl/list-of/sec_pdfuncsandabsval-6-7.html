Example 1.10.24. Absolute value of quadratic.

Consider \(f(x)=\abs{x^2-1}=\abs{g(x)}\text{,}\) where \(g(x)=x^2-1\text{.}\) Our usual sign diagram argument shows that \(g(x)< 0\) if and only if \(-1< x< 1\text{.}\) Thus we obtain the graph of \(f\) by flipping the part of the parabola \(g(x)=x^2-1\) lying over the interval \((-1,1)\) across the \(x\)-axis.

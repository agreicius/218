Example 1.4.20.

We can calcuate \(\dfrac{1}{6}+\dfrac{2}{3}\) using the formula above:
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{6}+\frac{2}{3} = \frac{(1)(3)+(6)(2)}{(6)(3)} = \frac{3+12}{18} = \frac{15}{18} = \frac{5}{6} \end{equation*}
but it is easier to convert to a common denominator of 6 instead:
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{6}+\frac{2}{3} = \frac{1}{6}+\frac{4}{6} = \frac{5}{6} \end{equation*}

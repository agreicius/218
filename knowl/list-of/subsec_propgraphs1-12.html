Solution.
  1. Using the point-slope formula with slope \(m=-2\) and point \((x_0,y_0)=(3,1)\text{,}\) we get the equation \(y = -2(x-3)+1\text{.}\) To put this equation into slope-intercept form, we just simplify:
    \begin{equation*} y = -2(x-3)+1\Rightarrow y=-2x+6+1 \Rightarrow y=-2x+7 \end{equation*}
    (The symbol \(\Rightarrow\) used in the calculation above denotes that the equality to the left of the symbol logically implies the equality to the right of the symbol.)

  2. The slope of the line through the points \((x_1,y_1) = (-1,2)\) and \((x_2,y_2) =(3,4)\) is \(\frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1} = \frac{4-2}{3-(-1)} =\frac{2}{4} = \frac{1}{2}\text{.}\) Using this slope, the point \((-1,2)\text{,}\) and the two-point formula, we get
    \begin{equation*} y = \frac{1}{2}(x-(-1))+2\Rightarrow y = \frac{1}{x}(x+1)+2 \Rightarrow y=\frac{1}{2}x+\frac{5}{2} \end{equation*}
    Using the point \((3,4)\) will give you the same equation in slope-intercept form after simplifying. Try it!

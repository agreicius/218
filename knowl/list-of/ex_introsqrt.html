Solution.
  1. We see that the \(x\)-values of points on the graph of \(g\) seem to range over all real numbers less than or equal to \(4\text{.}\) This suggests that the domain of \(g\) is \((-\infty, 4]\text{.}\) To confirm this observe that the expression \(\sqrt{4-x}\) is defined only if \(4-x\geq 0\text{.}\) Now solve this inequality for \(x\text{:}\)
    \begin{align*} 4-x \amp\geq 0 \\ 4-x +x \amp \geq 0+x\\ 4 \amp \geq x\text{.} \end{align*}
    We conclude that \(\sqrt{4-x}\) is defined if and only if \(x\leq 4\text{,}\) confirming that the domain of \(g\) is \((-\infty, 4]\text{.}\)

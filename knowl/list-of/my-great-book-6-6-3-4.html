Example 4.5.1. Non-example: The Derivative Does NOT Distribute Over a Product.

We can calculate the derivative of \(x^2\cdot x^3\) by simplifying:
\begin{equation*} x^2\cdot x^3 = x^5 \end{equation*}
and then taking the derivative:
\begin{equation*} \frac{d}{dx}(x^2\cdot x^3) = \frac{d}{dx}(x^5) = 5x^4\text{.} \end{equation*}

However, if we try to multiply the derivatives \(\frac{d}{dx}(x^2) = 2x\) and \(\frac{d}{dx}(x^3) = 3x^2\text{,}\) we get
\begin{equation*} \frac{d}{dx}(x^2)\cdot \frac{d}{dx}(x^3) = (2x)\cdot (3x^2) = 6x^3\text{.} \end{equation*}

So we can clearly see that
\begin{equation*} \frac{d}{dx}(x^2\cdot x^3) \neq \frac{d}{dx}(x^2)\cdot \frac{d}{dx}(x^3) \end{equation*}

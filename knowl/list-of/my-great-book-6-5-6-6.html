Example 4.4.9.

For example, derivatives of sums or constant multiples of the functions \(f(x)=x^2\) and \(g(x) = x^4\) can be expressed as sums or constant multiples of their derivatives \(f'(x) = 2x\) and \(g'(x) = 4x^3\text{,}\) as we can see here:
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}\frac{d}{dx}(5x^2+2x^4)\amp = \frac{d}{dx}(5x^2)+\frac{d}{dx}(2x^4) \amp \leftarrow\text{ sum rule } \\ \amp = 5\frac{d}{dx}(x^2)+2\frac{d}{dx}(x^4)\amp \leftarrow\text{ constant multiple rule } \\ \amp = 5(2x)+2(4x^3)\amp \leftarrow\text{ power rule } \\ \amp =10x+8x^3.\amp \leftarrow\text{ simplify } \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

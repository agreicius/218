  2. We will do this two ways. The first uses the chain rule:
    \begin{align*} g'(t) \amp =\frac{d}{dt}\ln(t^{1/3})\\ \amp = \frac{1}{t^{1/3}}\cdot (t^{1/3})'\\ \amp = \frac{1}{t^{1/3}}\cdot \frac{1}{3t^{2/3}}\\ \amp = \frac{1}{3t}\text{.} \end{align*}
    The expression at the end is surprisingly simple, until you realize that we could have just as well used some logarithmic properties to avoid the chain rule altogether:
    \begin{align*} \frac{d}{dt}g(t) \amp = \frac{d}{dt} \ln t^{1/3}\\ \amp = \frac{d}{dt} \left(\frac{1}{3}\ln t\right) \amp (\ln a^b=b\ln a)\\ \amp = \frac{1}{3}\cdot \frac{1}{t}\\ \amp = \frac{1}{3t}\text{.} \end{align*}
    Moral: as usual, keep an eye out for potential shortcuts through algebraic simplification!

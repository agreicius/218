We are told that the balloon is "being inflated at a constant rate of 44 cubic inches per second," which translates to \(\frac{dV}{dt} = 44\text{.}\) We wish to find "how fast the radius of the balloon is increasing," which translates to wanting to find \(\frac{dr}{dt}\text{.}\)
ðŸ”—
An equation relating the volume and radius of a sphere is
\begin{equation*} V=\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\text{.} \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
Differentiating both sides of the equation with respect to \(t\) (using implicit differentiation):
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}\frac{d}{dt}[V] \amp = \frac{d}{dt}\left[\frac{4}{3} \pi r^3\right]\\ \frac{dV}{dt} \amp = \frac{4}{3}\pi \left(3r^2\,\frac{dr}{dt}\right)\\ \frac{dV}{dt} \amp = 4\pi r^2\,\frac{dr}{dt} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
Since we wish to find \(\frac{dr}{dt}\text{,}\) letâ€™s solve for it. This isnâ€™t absolutely necessary, but it will make what follows easier to understand:
\begin{equation*} \frac{dr}{dt} = \frac{dV/dt}{4\pi r^2} \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
Part (a): We wish to find \(\frac{dr}{dt}\) at the "instant that the balloon has a radius of 4 inches," meaning when \(r=4\text{.}\) Since we also know \(\frac{dV}{dt} = 44\text{,}\) we can just plug these in:
\begin{equation*} \frac{dr}{dt}\Big|_{r=4} = \frac{44}{4\pi (4)^2} \approx 0.2188 \text{ inches per second } \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
Part (b): We wish to find \(\frac{dr}{dt}\) at the "instant that the balloon contains 100 cubic inches of air," meaning when \(V=100\text{.}\) This we cannot just plug in, since we need to know the value of \(r\) for this value of \(V\text{.}\) Hence we use the original equation \(V=\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\) to find the corresponding \(r\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}100\amp = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\\ \frac{100}{(4/3)\pi} \amp = r^3\\ \left(\frac{100}{(4/3)\pi}\right)^{1/3} \amp =r\\ 2.879 \approx r \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
Now we can solve for \(\frac{dr}{dt}\text{,}\) remembering that \(\frac{dV}{dt} = 44\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} \frac{dr}{dt}\Big|_{V=100} = \frac{dr}{dt}\Big|_{r\approx 2.879} = \frac{44}{4\pi(2.879)}\approx 0.422\text{ inches per second } \end{equation*}

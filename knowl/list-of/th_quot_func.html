Theorem 1.6.27. Quotients of functions.

Assume \(f\) is a function of the form
\begin{equation*} f(x)=\frac{g(x)}{h(x)}\text{,} \end{equation*}
where \(g\) and \(h\) are functions.
  1. The implied domain \(D\) of \(f\) is the set of \(x\in \R\) for which (a) \(g(x)\) and \(h(x)\) are both defined, and (b) \(h(x)\ne 0\text{.}\)

  2. The set \(Z\) of zeros of \(f\) is the set of zeros of \(g\) that lie in \(D\text{:}\) i.e.,
    \begin{equation*} Z=\{x\in D\mid g(x)=0\}\text{.} \end{equation*}

    \begin{equation} f(x)=0 \iff g(x)=0 \text{ and } h(x)\ne 0\text{.}\tag{1.48} \end{equation}

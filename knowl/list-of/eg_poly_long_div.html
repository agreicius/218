Example 1.6.12. Polynomial long division.

Find the quotient \(q(x)\) and remainder \(r(x)\) upon dividing \(f(x)=3x^4+2x^2+7x+1\) by \(g(x)=x^2+x+1\text{.}\)

Solution.
You may remember that long division of \(f\) by \(g\) proceeds iteratively, where at each step we multiply \(g\) by a monomial \(cx^m\) in order to match the leading term of the polynomial being divided. We spell this out in more detail in the steps below, but you are probably more accustomed to steps in this process being shown as in TableÂ 1.6.13, and indeed that is the way you should perform this operation. Consider the following as just an in-depth description of what we are actually doing in that process.
  • Multiply \(g\) by a monomial \(cx^n\) so that the leading terms of \(f\) and \((cx^n)g(x)\) match. In this case we choose the monomial \(3x^2\text{.}\)
    ðŸ”—
    Set our temporary quotient \(q(x)\) as \(3x^2\) and our temporary remainder \(r(x)\) as
    \begin{align*} r(x) \amp = f(x)-3x^2\cdot g(x)\\ \amp =f(x)-3x^2(x^2+x+1)\\ \amp =-3x^3-x^2+7x\text{.} \end{align*}

  • If \(\deg r\) is not less than \(\deg g\) for our temporary remainder \(r(x)\text{,}\) repeat Step 1 with \(g(x)\) and \(r(x)\) (instead of \(g(x)\) and \(f(x)\)). Update our temporary quotient \(q(x)\) by adding to it the relevant monomial \(cx^m\text{,}\) and update our temporary remainder \(r(x)\) by subtracting \((cx^m)g(x)\) from it.
    ðŸ”—
    In the present case, since
    \begin{equation*} \deg r(x)=\deg -3x^3-x^2+7x-3\geq \deg f=2 \text{,} \end{equation*}
    we multiply \(g(x)\) by the monomial \(-3x\) in order to match the leading term of \(-3x^3-x^2+7x-3\text{,}\) and update our \(q(x)\) and \(r(x)\) as follows:
    \begin{align*} q(x) \amp = 3x^2-3x\\ r(x) \amp = -3x^3-x^2+7x-3-(-3x)g(x)\\ \amp =-3x^3-x^2+7x-3-(-3x)(x^2+x+1) \\ \amp = 2x^2+10x+1 \end{align*}

  • Continue this updating process until \(\deg r< \deg g(x)\text{.}\)
    ðŸ”—
    In our case, we need to do one more iteration. To match the leading terms, we multiply \(g(x)\) by the monomial \(2\text{.}\) Updating our \(q(x)\) and \(r(x)\) yields
    \begin{align*} q(x) \amp =3x^2-3x+2\\ r(x) \amp = 2x^2+10x+1-2g(x)\\ \amp = 2x^2+10x+1-2(x^2+x+1)\\ \amp = 8x-1\text{.} \end{align*}
    Since \(\deg 8x-1=1< \deg g=2\text{,}\) we are done. We conclude that \(f(x)=g(x)q(x)+r(x)\text{,}\) where
    \begin{align*} q(x) \amp =3x^2-3x+2\\ r(x) \amp =8x-1\text{.} \end{align*}

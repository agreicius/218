Definition 1.1.34. Graph of equation.

Consider an arbitrary equation
\begin{equation} F(x,y)=c\text{,}\tag{1.9} \end{equation}
where \(F(x,y)\) is a mathematical expression in the unknowns \(x\) and \(y\text{,}\) and \(c\in \R\) is a fixed constant. A solution to (1.9) is a pair \((a,b)\in \R^2\) such that evaluating the expression (1.9) at \(x=a\) and \(y=b\) yields the true equation
\begin{equation*} F(a,b)=c\text{.} \end{equation*}
The graph of an equation like (1.9) is the set \(S\) of all solutions to this equation: i.e.,
\begin{equation*} S=\{(a,b)\in \R^2\mid F(a,b)=c\}\text{.} \end{equation*}

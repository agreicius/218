Solution.
  1. Graphically, for such lines \(L\text{,}\) we see that \(L\) intersects \(C\) in exactly one point. In more detail, if \(L\) has equation \(x=t\) with \(t\in (-\infty, 4]\text{,}\) then \(L\) intersects \(C\) at the point
    \begin{equation*} (t,f(t))=(t,\sqrt{4-t})\text{.} \end{equation*}
    We conclude that \(C\) passes the \(L\)-test for such a line \(L\text{.}\)

Example 8.6.8.

Calculate
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to -1^+}\frac{\sin^{-1}x}{\tan^{-1}x} \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
Solution.
Both \(\sin^{-1}x\) and \(\tan^{-1}x\) are defined to the right of and at the point \(x=-1\text{,}\) so we can solve this limit by evaluation. We have \(\sin^{-1}(-1) = -\frac{\pi}{2}\text{,}\) since \(-\frac{\pi}{2}\) is the unique angle in \(\left[-\frac{\pi}{2},\frac{\pi}{2}\right]\) whose sine is \(-1\text{.}\) We also have \(\tan^{-1}(-1) = -\frac{\pi}{4}\text{,}\) since \(-\frac{\pi}{4}\) is the unique angle in \(\left(-\frac{\pi}{2},\frac{\pi}{2}\right)\) whose tangent is \(-1\text{.}\) Therefore
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to -1^+} \frac{\sin^{-1}x}{\tan^{-1}x} = \frac{-\pi/2}{-\pi/4} = 2\text{.} \end{equation*}

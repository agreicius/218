  8. Before we move on to the second derivative, notice that calculating derivatives of rational functions is a significant challenge, especially since we would like to be able to factor the result. Lacking more advanced algebraic techniques for factoring, a key skill to develop is to take derivatives in such a way that the result is left as factored as possible. In particular, we do not want to expand the denominator of \(h'(x)\) before taking its derivative. In fact, we will leave it fully factored and instead opt to use the product rule when taking the derivative of the denominator:
    \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}h''(x) \amp = \frac{(x+1)^2(x-2)^2\cdot (-8x)-(-4(x^2+2)\cdot \frac{d}{dx}\left[(x+1)^2(x-2)^2\right]}{\left((x+1)^2(x-2)^2\right)^2}\\ \amp = \frac{-8x(x+1)^2(x-2)^2+4(x^2+2)\cdot \frac{d}{dx}\left[(x+1)^2(x-2)^2\right]}{(x+1)^4(x-2)^4} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
    Taking the derivative of the denominator and simplifying separately will make this computation much less painful:
    \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}\frac{d}{dx}\left[(x+1)^2(x-2)^2\right] \amp = 2(x+1)(x-2)^2+2(x+1)^2(x-1)\\ \amp = 2(x+1)(x-2)\big((x-2)+(x+1)\big)\\ \amp = 2(x+1)(x-2)(2x-1) \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
    Returning to calculating \(h''(x)\) and factoring as much as possible:

Since profit is revenue minus cost, the profit is represented by the vertical distance between the cost and revenue curves, marked by the vertical arrows. When revenue is below cost, the company is taking a loss; when revenue is above cost, the company is making a profit. The maximum profit must occur between about \(q=70\) and \(q=200\text{,}\) which is the interval in which the company is making a profit. Profit is maximized when the vertical distance between the curves is largest (and revenue is above cost). This occurs at approximately \(q=140\text{.}\)

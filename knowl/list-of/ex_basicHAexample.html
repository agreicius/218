Example 3.6.14.

The limit
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{x}{2x-1} = \frac{\infty}{\infty} \end{equation*}
is indeterminate. Use a table of values to estimate \(\ds\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{x}{2x-1}\text{.}\)

As \(x\) grows larger, the quantity \(\frac{x}{2x-1}\) appears to approach \(0.5 = \frac{1}{2}\text{,}\) so assuming that the pattern in the table continues for even larger values of \(x\text{,}\) we can conjecture that
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{x}{2x-1} = \frac{1}{2} \end{equation*}

Example 6.5.22.

Calculate \(\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}[\log_2(x+1)-\log_2(x^2+1)]\text{.}\)

Solution.
This limit is of the indeterminate form \(\infty-\infty\text{,}\) since as \(x\to\infty\text{,}\) both \(\log_2(x+1)\) and \(\log_2(x^2+1)\) approach \(\infty\text{.}\) Luckily, using the algebraic rules of logarithms, we can rewrite the limit in an alternative form with a fraction, as follows:
\begin{equation*} \displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}[\log_2(x+1)-\log_2(x^2+1)]=\displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}\log_2\left(\frac{x+1}{x^2+1}\right) \end{equation*}

As \(x\to\infty\text{,}\) the rational function \(\dfrac{x+1}{x^2+1}\) approaches \(0\) and is positive, which in turn means that the quantity \(\log_2\left(\frac{x+1}{x^2+1}\right)\) approaches \(-\infty\text{.}\) Therefore,
\begin{equation*} \displaystyle\lim_{x\to\infty}[\log_2(x+1)-\log_2(x^2+1)]=-\infty \end{equation*}

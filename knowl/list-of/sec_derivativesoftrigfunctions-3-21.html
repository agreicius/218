Example 8.7.11.

The derivative of \(f(x) = \cos(x^3)\) is
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}f'(x) \amp = -\sin(x^3)\cdot\frac{d}{dx}[x^3] \\ \amp = -\sin(x^3)\cdot(3x^2) \\ \amp = -3x^2\sin(x^3). \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

In order to find the second derivative, we need the product rule since \(-3x^2\) and \(\sin(x^3)\) are two functions that are being multiplied:
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}f''(x) \amp = -3x^2\frac{d}{dx}[\sin(x^3)]+\sin(x^3)\frac{d}{dx}[-3x^2]\\ \amp = -3x^2(-\cos(x^3)\cdot(3x^2))+\sin(x^3)(-6x) \\ \amp = 9x^4\cos(x^3)-6x\sin(x^3). \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

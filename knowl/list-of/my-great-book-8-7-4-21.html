Example 6.6.21.

Find the derivative of
\begin{equation*} g(x) = \sqrt{x}e^{x^2} \end{equation*}

Using the product and chain rules, we have
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}\frac{d}{dx}(\sqrt{x}) \amp = \sqrt{x}\cdot \frac{d}{dx}(e^{x^2})+e^{x^2}\cdot\frac{d}{dx}(\sqrt{x})\\ \amp =\sqrt{x}\cdot e^{x^2}(2x)+e^{x^2}\cdot \frac{1}{2}x^{-1/2}\\ \amp = 2x^{3/2}e^{x^2}+\frac{e^{x^2}}{2\sqrt{x}}\\ \amp = \left(2x^{3/2}+\frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}\right)e^{x^2} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Example 6.7.10.

Compute the derivative of \(f(x)=\ln(kx)\text{,}\) where \(k\) is a constant.
ðŸ”—
Solution.
We could use log rules to rewrite \(f(x)=\ln(kx)=\ln k+\ln x\text{,}\) and see that
\begin{equation*} f'(x)=0+\frac{1}{x}=\frac{1}{x} \end{equation*}

If we use the chain rule, we get the same result:
\begin{equation*} \frac{d}{dx}\left(\ln (kx)\right)=\frac{1}{kx}\cdot k=\frac{k}{kx}=\frac{1}{x} \end{equation*}

Example 4.5.9.

If \(h(x) = \sqrt{x}\ g(x)\) and we know that \(g(4) = 7\) and \(g'(4) = 3\text{,}\) we can still find \(h'(4)\text{.}\)
ðŸ”—
Applying the product rule, we get
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}h'(x) \amp = \frac{d}{dx}\left[\sqrt{x}\ g(x)\right]\\ \amp = \frac{d}{dx}\left[\sqrt{x}\right]\cdot g(x)+ \sqrt{x}\cdot \frac{d}{dx}\left[g(x)\right]\\ \amp =\frac{1}{2}x^{-1/2}\cdot g(x)+\sqrt{x}\cdot g'(x)\\ \amp = \frac{g(x)}{2\sqrt{x}}+\sqrt{x}\ g'(x) \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
Now we can evaluate at \(x=4\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} h'(4) = \frac{g(4)}{2\sqrt{4}}+\sqrt{4}\ g'(4) = \frac{7}{2(2)}+2(3) = \frac{7}{4}+6 = \frac{31}{4} \end{equation*}

Example 4.5.5.

For example, with our earlier example of \(f(x) = x^2+1\) and \(g(x) = x^3\text{,}\) we can differentiate \(f(x)g(x)\) without multiplying it out first:
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}\frac{d}{dx}(f(x)g(x)) \amp = f'(x) g(x)+f(x) g'(x) \amp \leftarrow\text{ product rule } \\ \amp = (2x)(x^3) +(x^2+1)(3x^2)\amp \leftarrow f'(x) = 2x, \text{ and } g(x)=3x^2\\ \amp = 2x^4+3x^4+3x^2 \amp \leftarrow\text{ algebra } \\ \amp = 5x^4+3x^2 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

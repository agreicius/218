Example 8.7.19.

Calculate the derivative of \(g(x) = \csc(2x)\text{.}\)
ðŸ”—
Solution.
This function is a composition of \(g(u) = \csc(u)\) with \(u=2x\text{.}\) Recall that
\begin{equation*} g'(u) = -\csc u\cot u \end{equation*}
and so by the chain rule,
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}g'(x) \amp = -\csc(2x)\cot(2x)\cdot\frac{d}{dx}(2x) \\ \amp = -\csc(2x)\cot(2x)\cdot 2 \\ \amp = -2\csc(2x)\cot(2x). \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
Note that we have to include the \(2x\) in both factors of the derivative \(g'(u) = -\csc u\cot u\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} f(x) = \begin{cases}x+1,\amp \text{ if } x\lt -2\\ (x+1)^2,\amp \text{ if } -2\leq x\leq 1\\ 2-x,\amp \text{ if } x>1 \end{cases} \end{equation*}
is positive and negative.

Solution.
The piecewise-defined function \(f\) can be discontinuous only at its break points \(x=-2\) and \(x=1\text{.}\) We check whether the left and right limits at each point are the same.
  • At \(x=-2\text{,}\) we use the piece \(x+1\) to the left and the piece \((x+1)^2\) to the right:
    \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}\lim_{x\to -2^-}f(x) \amp = \lim_{x\to -2^-}x+1 = -2+1 = -1\\ \lim_{x\to -2^+}f(x) \amp =\lim_{x\to -2^+}(x+1)^2 = (-2+1)^2 = (-1)^2 = 1 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
    The left and right limits are different, so the function is discontinuous at \(x=-2\text{;}\) it has a jump.

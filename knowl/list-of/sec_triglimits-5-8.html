Example 8.6.16.

Calculate
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{\ln x}{\tan^{-1}x} \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
Solution.
As \(x\to\infty\text{,}\) \(\ln x\to\infty\) and \(\tan^{-1}x\to\frac{\pi}{2}\text{.}\) Therefore the limit in question has the form \(\frac{\infty}{\pi/2}\text{,}\) which is infinite. Thus
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to \infty}\frac{\ln x}{\tan^{-1}x} = \infty \end{equation*}

Example 4.5.17.

Let \(y=\dfrac{x^2+x-2}{x^3+6}\text{.}\) There is no algebra that will make this expression simpler in order to make taking the derivative easier. Thus using the quotient rule, we have
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}y' \amp = \frac{(x^3+6)\frac{d}{dx}(x^2+x-2) - (x^2+x-2)\frac{d}{dx}(x^3+6)}{(x^3+6)^2}\\ \amp =\frac{(x^3+6)(2x+1) - (x^2+x-2)(3x^2)}{(x^3+6)^2}\\ \amp = \frac{(2x^4+x^3+12x+6)-(3x^4+3x^3-6x^2)}{(x^3+6)^2}\\ \amp = \frac{-x^4-2x^3+6x^2+12x+6}{(x^3+6)^2} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

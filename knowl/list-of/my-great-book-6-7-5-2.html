Theorem 4.6.2. The Chain Rule.

Suppose that \(f(u(x))\) is a composition of functions. Then for all values of \(x\) at which \(u\) is differentiable at \(x\) and \(f\) is differentiable at \(u(x)\text{,}\) the derivative of \(f\) with respect to \(x\) is equal to the product of the derivative of \(f\) with respect to \(u\) and the derivative of \(u\) with respect to \(x\text{.}\) In Leibniz notation, we write this as
\begin{equation*} \frac{df}{dx}=\frac{df}{du}\cdot\frac{du}{dx} \end{equation*}
🔗
In “prime” notation, we write it as
\begin{equation*} (f\circ u)'(x)=f'(u(x))\cdot u'(x) \end{equation*}

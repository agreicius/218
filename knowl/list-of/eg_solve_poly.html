Solution.
First bring the equation in to the form (1.46):
\begin{align*} x^3-2x+3\amp =x+1\amp \\ x^3-3x+2 \amp = 0 \text{.} \end{align*}
Luckily, TheoremÂ 1.5.15 helps us find an easy integer root of the polynomial on the left, allowing us to factor (in two steps) as
\begin{equation*} x^3-3x+2=(x-1)(x^2+x-2)=(x-1)(x-1)(x+2)\text{.} \end{equation*}
From the factored equation
\begin{equation*} (x-1)(x-1)(x+2)=0 \end{equation*}
we conclude that the solutions to the original equation are \(x=1\) (a repeated root here) and \(x=-2\text{.}\)

Corollary 1.5.25. Domain and zeros of rational functions.

Let \(f(x)=p(x)/q(x)\text{,}\) where \(p\) and \(q\) are polynomials.
  1. The implied domain \(D\) of \(f\) is
    \begin{equation*} D=\{x\in \R\mid q(x)\ne 0\}\text{.} \end{equation*}

  2. The set \(Z\) of zeros of \(f\) is the set of zeros of \(p\) that are not zeros of \(q\text{:}\) i.e.,
    \begin{equation*} Z=\{x\in \R\mid p(x)=0 \text{ and } q(x)\ne 0\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
    Equivalently, using logical shorthand:
    \begin{equation} f(x)=0 \iff p(x)=0 \text{ and } q(x)\ne 0\text{.}\tag{1.49} \end{equation}

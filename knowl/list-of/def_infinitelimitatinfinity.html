Definition 3.4.13. Infinite Limit at Infinity.

If the values of a function \(f(x)\) grow without bound as \(x\) grows without bound, then we say that \(\infty\) is the limit of \(f(x)\) as \(x\) approaches \(\infty\) and we write
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to\infty} f(x) = \infty \end{equation*}

\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to\infty} f(x) = -\infty \end{equation*}

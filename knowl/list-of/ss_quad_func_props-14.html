Example 1.2.29.

For example, the expression \(x^2-3x-4\) can be factored as \((x-4)(x+1)\text{.}\) Breaking this down, observe that the first terms in both \(x-4\) and \(x+1\) are \(x\) and multiply to the \(x^2\text{.}\) The outside terms \(x\) and \(1\) multiply to \(x\) while the inside terms \(-4\) and \(x\) multiply to \(-4x\text{,}\) and that these can be added together to get \(-3x\text{.}\) The last terms \(-4\) and \(1\) multiply to \(-4\text{.}\)

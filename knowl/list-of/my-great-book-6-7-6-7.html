Example 4.6.11.

The derivative of
\begin{equation*} y=\sqrt[3]{5-2x} \end{equation*}
is
\begin{equation*} y' = \frac{1}{3}(5-2x)^{-2/3}(-2) = \frac{-2}{(5-2x)^{2/3}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
where the outside function is \(f(u) = \sqrt[3]{u} = u^{1/3}\) with derivative \(f'(u) = \frac{1}{3}u^{-2/3}\) and the inside function is \(u(x) = 5-2x\) with derivative \(u'(x) = -2\text{.}\)

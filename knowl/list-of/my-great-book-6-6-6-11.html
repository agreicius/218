Using the quotient rule, we need to know that \(\frac{d}{dx}(x^7+\sqrt{x}) = 7x^6+\frac{1}{2}x^{-1/2}\) and \(\frac{d}{dx} (x^3)= 3x^2\) so that
\begin{equation*} h'(x) = \frac{(x^3)\cdot \left(7x^6+\frac{1}{2}x^{-1/2}\right) - \left(x^7+\sqrt{x}\right)\cdot (3x^2)}{(x^3)^2} \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
This is a bit of a mess, but we can simplify it by first distributing and combining like terms in the numerator, then distributing the denominator and simplifying powers of \(x\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}h'(x) \amp = \frac{7x^9+\frac{1}{2}x^{5/2} - 3x^9-3x^{5/2}}{x^6}\\ \amp = \frac{4x^9-\frac{5}{2}x^{5/2}}{x^6}\\ \amp =\frac{4x^9}{x^6}-\frac{\frac{5}{2}x^{5/2}}{x^6}\\ \amp = 4x^3 - \frac{5}{2}x^{-7/2} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

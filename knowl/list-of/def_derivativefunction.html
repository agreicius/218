Definition 4.2.10. The Derivative of a Function.

The derivative of a function \(f(x)\) is the function \(f\,'(x)\) defined by
\begin{equation*} f\,'(x)=\frac{df}{dx} = \displaystyle\lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h} \end{equation*}

In terms of the change in the \(x\)- and \(f\)-values, we can also write
\begin{equation*} f\,'(x) = \lim_{\Delta x\to 0}\frac{\Delta f}{\Delta x}\text{,} \end{equation*}
meaning that the derivative is the limit of the change in \(f\) as the change in \(x\) becomes infinitely small. The domain of \(f\,'(x)\) is the set of values \(x\) for which the defining limit of \(f\,'(x)\) exists.

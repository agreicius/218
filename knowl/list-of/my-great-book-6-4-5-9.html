Example 4.3.24.

For example, since the tangent line to \(f(x) = x^2\) at \(x=3\) is the line \(y=6x-9\text{,}\) the linear equation \(L(x)=6x-9\) can be used as a rough approximation to the graph of the function \(f(x) = x^2\text{,}\) at least for values of \(x\) close to 3. This approximation will be better the closer we are to \(x=3\text{.}\) As the following figure shows, the two graphs have nearly the same height at the point \(x=3.5\) and are still relatively close even at \(x=4\text{.}\)

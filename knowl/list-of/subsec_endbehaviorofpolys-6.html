Example 3.6.9.

On the other hand,
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to-\infty} x^2-x^3 = \left(\lim_{x\to-\infty} x^2\right) - \left(\lim_{x\to-\infty} x^3\right) = \infty-(-\infty) = \infty+\infty\text{,} \end{equation*}
which is NOT indeterminate, since we can understand the last expression as adding two large, positive numbers, which results in an even larger positive number, hence:
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to-\infty} x^2-x^3 = \infty+\infty = \infty \end{equation*}

Definition 1.10.18. Distance function on \(\R\).

Let \(a,b\in \R\text{.}\) We define the distance \(d(a,b)\) between \(a\) and \(b\) to be
\begin{equation} d(a,b)=\abs{a-b}=\abs{b-a}\text{.}\tag{1.67} \end{equation}
Geometrically, \(d(a,b)\) is the length of the line segment between \(a\) and \(b\text{,}\) considered as points on the real line.

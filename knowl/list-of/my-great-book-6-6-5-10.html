Example 4.5.8.

Find the derivative of \(q(t)\cdot r(t)\) given that \(q(t) = 4t^3-2t^7+5\) and \(r(t) = \frac{1}{t^3}+ \frac{1}{t}-15\text{.}\)
ðŸ”—
Solution.
While we could simplify the product \(q(t)\cdot r(t)\text{,}\) it would be a headache. Instead, we compute the derivative via the product rule. First, we calculate the individual derivatives:
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}q'(t) \amp = 12t^2-14t^6\\ r'(t) \amp = -\frac{3}{t^4}-\frac{1}{t^2} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
so that we can plug them into the product rule:
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}\frac{d}{dx}[q(t)\cdot r(t)]\amp = q'(t) r(t)+q(t) r'(t)\\ \amp = (12t^2-14t^6)\left( \frac{1}{t^3}+ \frac{1}{t}-15\right)+(4t^3-2t^7+5)\left(-\frac{3}{t^4}-\frac{1}{t^2}\right) \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

We will not attempt to simplify this last expression. WeBWorK will accept it as it is.

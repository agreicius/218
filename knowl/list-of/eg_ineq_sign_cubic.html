Solution.
First bring the inequality into the standard form
\begin{equation*} f(x)=x^4+x^2-2> 0\text{.} \end{equation*}
The quartic polynomial \(f\) has no points where it is undefined. We factor \(f\) to find its roots. We have
\begin{align*} f(x) \amp =x^4+x^2-2\\ \amp =u^2+u-2 \amp (u=x^2)\\ \amp =(u+2)(u-1)\\ \amp =(x^2+2)(x^2-1)\\ \amp =(x^2+2)(x-1)(x+1)\text{.} \end{align*}
Since \(x^2+2\) is never equal to zero, we see that the roots of \(f\) are \(x=-1, 1\text{.}\) Using the test points
\begin{align*} f(-2) \amp = 18> 0\\ f(0) \amp = -2< 0 \\ f(2) \amp = 18> 0 \text{,} \end{align*}

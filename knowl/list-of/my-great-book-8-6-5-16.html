Example 6.5.15.

Consider the function \(h(x) = \log_2(x^2-1)\text{.}\) This can be viewed as a composition \(f\circ g\) where \(f(x) = \log_2 x\) and \(g(x) = x^2-1\text{.}\)
ðŸ”—
The argument of the logarithm in \(h(x) = \log_2(x^2-1)\) is the expression \(x^2-1\text{,}\) and the values of this argument determine whether \(h(x)\) has a vertical asymptote
ðŸ”—
We have established that \(f(x) = \log_2x\) has a vertical asymptote at \(x=0\) for \(x>0\text{.}\)
ðŸ”—
\(g(x) = x^2-1=0\) for \(x=\pm 1\) and we can check via a sign chart that \(x^2-1>0\) for \(x\lt -1\) and for \(x>1\text{.}\)
ðŸ”—
Thus the composite function \(h(x) = f(g(x) = \log_2(x^2-1)\) will have vertical asymptotes at \(x=-1\) and \(x=1\text{,}\) but only on the sides where \(x^2-1>0\text{,}\) meaning from the left of \(x=-1\) and the right of \(x=1\text{.}\)

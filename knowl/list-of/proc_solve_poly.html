Procedure 1.5.22. Solving polynomial equations.

Consider a polynomial equation of the form
\begin{equation} \anpoly =b\text{.}\tag{1.45} \end{equation}
To solve this equation for \(x\text{,}\) proceed as follows.
  1. Use algebra to bring all terms to the left side (using addition/subtraction), obtaining an equation of the form
    \begin{equation} a_nx^n+a_{n-1}x^{n-1}+\cdots +(a_0-b)=0\text{.}\tag{1.46} \end{equation}

Example 6.4.22.

An earthquake is measured with a wave amplitude 392 times as great as \(A_0\text{.}\) What is the magnitude of this earthquake using the Richter scale, to the nearest tenth?

Solution.
Since \(A\) is 392 times as large as \(A_0\text{,}\) \(A = 392A_0\text{.}\) Substituting this expression in for \(A\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} R=\log_{10}\left(\frac{392A_0}{A_0}\right) = \log_{10}(392)\approx 2.6\text{.} \end{equation*}

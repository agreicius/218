Procedure 2.16.19. Conversion between degrees and radians.

Let \(\theta\) be an angle, let \(s\) be its measure in radians, and let \(t\) be its measure in degrees. We have
\begin{align} s \amp =\frac{\pi}{180}\,t\tag{2.55}\\ t \amp =\frac{180}{\pi}\,s\text{.}\tag{2.56} \end{align}
In other words, we think of the ratios \(180/\pi\) and \(\pi/180\) as conversion factors corresponding to the relation
\begin{align} 180 \text{ degrees} \amp = \pi \text{ radians}\text{.}\tag{2.57} \end{align}
Using unit arithmetic we can remember the conversion equations above as follows:
\begin{align*} x \text{ radians} \amp = x \cancel{\text{ radians}}\cdot \frac{180 \text{ degrees}}{\pi \cancel{\text{ radians}}}=\frac{180}{\pi}x \text{ degrees}\\ x \text{ degrees} \amp = x \cancel{\text{ degrees}}\cdot \frac{\pi \text{ radians}}{180 \cancel{\text{ degrees}}}=\frac{\pi}{180}x \text{ radians} \end{align*}

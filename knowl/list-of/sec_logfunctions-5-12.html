We then have
\begin{equation*} t = \log_{0.88}(0.2)\text{.} \end{equation*}
By the Change of Base Formula, we can rewrite this as a fraction using any base logarithm, including but not limited to base-10:
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}\log_{0.88}(0.2) \amp = \frac{\log_{10}(0.2)}{\log_{10}(0.88)}\\ \amp =\frac{\log_{2}(0.2)}{\log_{2}(0.88)}\\ \amp = \frac{\log_{b}(0.2)}{\log_{b}(0.88)} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
for any choice of \(b\) with \(b>0\) and \(b\neq 1\text{.}\)

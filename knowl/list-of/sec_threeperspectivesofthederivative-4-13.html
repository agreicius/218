Example 4.1.11. Estimating the slope of a tangent line with a sequence of secant lines.

Estimate the slope of the line tangent to the graph of \(f(x) = -\frac{1}{2}x^2+3x\) at the point \((2,f(2))\) by calculating a sequence of slopes of secant lines.
ðŸ”—
Solution.
The tangent line passes through the point \((2,f(2)) = (2,4)\) since \(f(2) = -\frac{1}{2}(2)^2+3(2)=4\text{.}\) It is shown in red in each of the graphs that follow. Since we only know one point on this line, we cannot compute its slope directly. We will approximate its slope by considering a sequence of slopes of secant lines on smaller and smaller intervals, namely \([2,3], [2,2.5], [2,2.25]\text{,}\) and \([2,2.1]\text{,}\) as shown in the graphs below:
ðŸ”—
ðŸ”—
In the definition of the derivative, we can think of this as a sequence of \(h\)-values \(h=1, h=0.5, h=0.25\text{,}\) and \(h=0.1\) approaching zero.
ðŸ”—
The slope of the secant line from \(x=2\) to \(x=3\) in the leftmost graph is given by
\begin{equation*} \frac{f(3)-f(2)}{3-2} = \frac{\left(-\frac{1}{2}(3)^2+3(3)\right)-\left(-\frac{1}{2}(2)^2+3(2)\right)}{3-2} = \frac{4.5-4}{3-2} = 0.5 \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
Similarly, the slopes of the remaining three secant lines are given by
\begin{equation*} \frac{f(2.5)-f(2)}{2.5-2}=0.75, \frac{f(2.25)-f(2)}{2.25-2}=0.875, \text{ and } \frac{f(2.1)-f(2)}{2.1-2}=0.95\text{.} \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
Each of these slopes is an approximation to the slope of the red tangent line. As the graphs shown suggest, we would expect this sequence of slopes to be getting closer and closer to the actual slope of the red tangent line; notice for example that, in the last figure shown, the green secant line is almost indistinguishable from the red tangent line.
ðŸ”—
In a similar fashion we can calculate the slopes of secant lines from the left of \(x=2\text{.}\) For example, the slope of the secant line from \(x=1\) to \(x=2\) is given by
\begin{equation*} \frac{f(1)-f(2)}{1-2} = \frac{\left(-\frac{1}{2}(1)^2+3(1)\right)-\left(-\frac{1}{2}(2)^2+3(2)\right)}{1-2} = \frac{2.5-4}{1-2} = 1.5\text{.} \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
Over the smaller intervals \([1,5,2], [1.75,2]\text{,}\) and \([1.9,2]\) we have secant lines with slopes given by
\begin{equation*} \frac{f(1.5)-f(2)}{1.5-2}=1.25, \frac{f(1.75)-f(2)}{1.75-2}=1.125, \text{ and } \frac{f(1.9)-f(2)}{1.9-2} = 1.05\text{.} \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—

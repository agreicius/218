Example 8.6.7.

Calculate
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to 0}\sqrt{\sin \left(x+\frac{\pi}{2}\right)} \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
Solution.
\(f(x) = \sqrt{\sin \left(x+\frac{\pi}{2}\right)}\) is a combination of functions that are continuous on their domains, and thus is continuous on its domain. Since \(x=0\) is in the domain of \(f\text{,}\) we can solve this limit by simple evaluation:
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}\lim_{x\to 0}\sqrt{\sin \left(x+\frac{\pi}{2}\right)} \amp = \sqrt{\sin \left(0+\frac{\pi}{2}\right)} \\ \amp =\sqrt{\sin \left(\frac{\pi}{2}\right)} \\ \amp =\sqrt{1} = 1. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

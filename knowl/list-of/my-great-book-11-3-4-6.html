Example 9.2.10.

Use the Squeeze Theorem to calculate
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{3x^2+\cos x^2}{x^2+1} \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
Solution.
Beginning with the inequality
\begin{equation*} -1\leq \cos \theta\leq 1\text{,} \end{equation*}
we build up the middle of the inequality until it is equal to the given function: \(\frac{3x^2+\cos x^2}{x^2+1}\text{.}\)
ðŸ”—
To start, we use \(\theta = x^2\) and have
\begin{equation*} -1\leq \cos x^2\leq 1 \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
Next, add \(3x^2\) to both sides of the inequality:
\begin{equation*} 3x^2-1\leq 3x^2+\cos x^2\leq 3x^2+1 \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
Lastly, divide all three parts by \(x^2+1\text{.}\) Note that the expression \(x^2+1\) is positive for all values of \(x\text{,}\) so we do not have to worry about flipping the inequality:
\begin{equation*} \frac{3x^2-1}{x^2+1}\leq \frac{3x^2+\cos x^2}{x^2+1}\leq \frac{3x^2+1}{x^2+1} \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
Now from our previous work with rational functions, we know that
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{3x^2-1}{x^2+1} =3 \text{ and } \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{3x^2+1}{x^2+1}=3 \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
Hence by the Squeeze Theorem,
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{3x^2+\cos x^2}{x^2+1}=3 \end{equation*}

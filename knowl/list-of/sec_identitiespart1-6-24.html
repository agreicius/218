Example 8.3.21.

For example, for every angle \(\theta\) we clearly have
\begin{equation*} \sin (\theta+2\pi) = \sin\theta\text{,} \end{equation*}
since \(\theta+2\pi\) and \(\theta\) have the same terminal edge and thus intersect the unit circle at the same point. This is the same as observing that the function \(\sin \theta\) has period \(2\pi\text{.}\) Likewise, we have
\begin{equation*} \cos(\theta+2\pi) = \cos\theta \end{equation*}

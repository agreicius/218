Example 1.2.40.

Applying the quadratic formula to the equation \(3x^2-7x+1=0\text{,}\) we have \(a=3\text{,}\) \(b=-7\text{,}\) and \(c=1\text{,}\) so the solutions are
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}x \amp = \frac{-(-7)\pm\sqrt{(-7)^2-4(3)(1)}}{2(3)}\\ \amp = \frac{7\pm\sqrt{49-12}}{6}\\ \amp =\frac{7\pm\sqrt{37}}{6} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
and so the quadratic function \(q(x)=3x^2-7x+1\) has two roots at \(x=\frac{7+\sqrt{37}}{6}\approx 2.18046\) and at \(x=\frac{7-\sqrt{37}}{6}\approx 0.152873\text{.}\) Once again, you should verify this using this Desmos graph

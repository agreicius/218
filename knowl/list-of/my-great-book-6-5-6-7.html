Example 4.4.10.

Find the derivative of \(f(z) = 4\sqrt{z}+\dfrac{3}{z^4}\text{.}\) Hint: rewrite both terms as powers of \(z\text{.}\)
ðŸ”—
Solution.
Rewriting in terms of powers of \(z\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} f(z) = 4z^{1/2}+3z^{-4} \end{equation*}
so that
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}f'(z) \amp = 4\left(\frac{1}{2}z^{1/2-1}\right)+3(-4z^{-4-1})\\ \amp = 2z^{-1/2}-12z^{-5}\\ \amp = \frac{2}{\sqrt{z}}-\frac{12}{z^5} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

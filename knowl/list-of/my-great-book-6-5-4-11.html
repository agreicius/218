For example, the volume of a cylinder is \(V=\pi r^2 h\text{.}\) If we think of the radius \(r\) of the cylinder as being a changeable number (meaning a variable) while the height \(h\) is constant, then the volume is a function of \(r\text{:}\) \(V(r) = \pi r^2 h\text{.}\) We can take its derivative:
\begin{equation*} V'(r) = \frac{d}{dr}(V) \end{equation*}

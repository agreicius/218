Theorem 2.18.17. Trigonometry of right triangles.

Let \(\theta\) be an acute angle of a right triangle, let \(\text{adj}\) and \(\text{opp}\) denote the lengths of the sides adjacent and opposite to \(\theta\text{,}\) and let \(\text{hyp}\) denote the length of the hypotenuse of the triangle. We have
\begin{align*} \cos\theta \amp = \frac{\text{adj}}{\text{hyp}} \amp \sin\theta\amp=\frac{\text{opp}}{\text{hyp}}\amp \tan\theta=\frac{\text{opp}}{\text{adj}}\\ \sec\theta \amp = \frac{\text{hyp}}{\text{adj}} \amp \csc\theta\amp=\frac{\text{hyp}}{\text{opp}}\amp \cot\theta=\frac{\text{adj}}{\text{opp}}\text{.} \end{align*}

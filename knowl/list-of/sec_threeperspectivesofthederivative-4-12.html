Definition 4.1.10.

The tangent line to a function \(f(x)\) at the point \(x=a\) is the line with slope \(m=f\,'(a)\) that passes through the point \((a,f(a))\text{,}\) provided that \(f\,'(a)\) exists. This can be visualized as the limit as \(h\to 0\) of secant lines through the points \((a,f(a))\) and \((a+h,f(a+h))\text{.}\)

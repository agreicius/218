Example 3.2.43.
We do not know how to calculate
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to -2} \frac{2-x}{4-x^2}\text{ or } \lim_{x\to 2} \frac{2-x}{4-x^2} \end{equation*}
since those \(x\)-values are not in the domain of \(\frac{2-x}{4-x^2}\text{.}\)

Lastly, since \(x^3+3\leq 0\) for \(x\leq -\sqrt[3]{3}\text{,}\) the expression \(\frac{1}{\sqrt{x^3+3}}\) is undefined, so we do not know how to calculate
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to -\sqrt[3]{3}^+} \frac{1}{\sqrt{x^3+3}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Note that for \(x\lt -\sqrt[3]{3}\text{,}\) the expression \(\frac{1}{\sqrt{x^3+3}}\) is undefined so any limit will simply fail to exist.

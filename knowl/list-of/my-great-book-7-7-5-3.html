Solution.
We can see that the first derivative changes sign at \(x=-1\) and \(x=2\text{.}\) Since it changes from negative to positive at \(x=-1\text{,}\) this is a local minimum. Since it changes from positive to negative at \(x=2\text{,}\) this is a local maximum. We cannot determine the \(y\)-values of the function at these two points, but we can infer from the "maximum/minimum" designations that the \(y\)-value at \(x=2\) is higher than the \(y\)-value at \(x=-1\text{.}\)

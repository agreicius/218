Example 9.4.14.

The limit
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to\infty}x^{1/x} \end{equation*}
has the indeterminate form \(\infty^0\) since \(x\to\infty\) and \(\frac{1}{x}\to 0\) as \(x\) approaches \(\infty\text{.}\) Taking \(\ln\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}\lim_{x\to\infty}\ln\left(x^{1/x}\right) \amp = \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{1}{x}\ln x \amp \leftarrow\text{ form } 0\cdot\infty\\ \amp = \lim_{x\to\infty} \frac{\ln x}{x} \amp \leftarrow\text{ form } \frac{\infty}{\infty}\\ \amp \stackrel[L'H]{}= \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{1/x}{1} \amp \leftarrow\text{ L'H\^opital's } \\ \amp = \lim_{x\to\infty} \frac{1}{x} \amp \leftarrow\text{ simplify } \\ \amp =0 \amp \leftarrow\text{ evaluate } \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
